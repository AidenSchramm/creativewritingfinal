Journals

Aiden Schramm
  • Unit 1A
    • Through the feedback I have received during the two times I took integrated composition, I can conclude that the quality of my writing is more or less sufficient. However I still have yet to be able to consistently deliver assignments by the due date. Though difficult, I would like to improve the consistency of when I turn in assignments by a significant amount.
  • Unit 1B
    • Being able to experience other stories was useful for getting ideas and strategies to incorporate into my own writing. Additionally, having my story read aloud was extremely helpful in my own evaluation of the stories overall flow and voice. Specifically, there are several locations where I overuse singular and plural forms of “it” and “they” where clarifying the subject would be more appropriate.
  • Unit 1C
    • Another aspect that could be improved is the use of emotive language to further draw in the reader. As it stands, a somewhat neutral tone is used throughout the narrative, with very little to which one could relate to emotionally. Overall, the improvements that could be made would take very little time and effort to complete, and will drastically improve the readability and overall flow of the story. All that remains to be done is to simply create an appropriate conclusion to wrap everything up.

Specifically, there are several locations where I overuse singular and plural forms of “it” and “they” where clarifying the subject would be more appropriate.
  • Unit 2C
    • Making good use of point of view to construct a narrative is very important in creating a coherent and enjoyable written work. Both in my everyday interactions and my writing, I feel as if I have a decent grasp on how others may perceive situations differently. At the same time, I often struggle to see things exclusively from my own perspective, instead incorporating several different sources of detail into what I experience. Obviously it is impossible to literally experience life as another person, yet merely entertaining the idea of experiencing the life of another is a conduit to fostering a connection through which one can write about the subjective experience of an individual.
